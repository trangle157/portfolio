PC Component Visualization

Executive Summary

Choosing the right components for your PC could be overwhelming and exciting. This PC build visualizer aims to help PC builders of different experiences, budget, and use case, to find the best combination that fits their needs and if an upgrade in parts is worthy of the budget increase.


The feature that is most challenging to the visualization is presenting a large amount of data in visually acceptable scales and reduce cognitive load for comparison and contrast.


Inspiration

Interested in building a PC in 2020, I looked for parts and components and opened multiple tabs at the same time reading up on tech articles, motherboard compatibility, and price. The process was more than rewarding seeing your PC turned on for the first time and seeing the BIOS screen showing up, feeling as if you have brought some thing to life.


While studying UX design at UC Berkeley Extension, I was particularly curious about data visualization and (color) accessibility, after incidentally falling in love with Math for for years in college. Having the PC build experience fresh in my gear, I decided to do a data visualization piece using the data I scraped from UserBenchmark.com.
